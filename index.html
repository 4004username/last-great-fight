The Last Great Fight

Chapter 1: The Monster in Decline

Mike Tyson was undefeated and feared, but behind the scenes, his life was unraveling. His split from Kevin Rooney broke ties with Cus D’Amato's legacy. Wild partying, fractured management, and fading focus plagued him. Tyson arrived in Tokyo for the 1990 fight untrained and overconfident — unaware that Buster Douglas was bringing more than fists to the ring.

Chapter 2: The Forgotten Challenger

James “Buster” Douglas had a reputation for inconsistency and quitting. But after his mother's death, something changed. Media ignored him, promoters doubted him, but Douglas trained with heart and purpose. He came to Tokyo not as a footnote — but to write a chapter in history.

Chapter 3: Tokyo Shockwave

February 11, 1990. Douglas dominated with jabs and movement. Tyson looked lost, bleeding, slow. In Round 8, Tyson knocked Douglas down — but he rose and returned with fury. In Round 10, Douglas unleashed a combination ending in a brutal uppercut. Tyson fell. For the first time ever, he couldn't rise before the count. The impossible had happened. Tyson was defeated.

Chapter 4: Ripples of Glory and Ruin

Tyson spiraled into legal issues and incarceration; Douglas tasted glory but faded after a loss to Holyfield. The sport was forever changed. Joe Layden’s book doesn’t just detail a fight — it captures emotion, heartbreak, redemption, and a night that became boxing legend.
